Historic Trends

Historic trends of some currencies against the United States Dollar

EUR against USD

The EUR has seen fluctuations against the USD, with periods of depreciation and recovery influenced by economic and political factors in the Eurozone.

GBP against USD

The GBP has experienced volatility against the USD, notably affected by Brexit and economic policy changes, reflecting market reactions.

CNY against USD

The CNY has shown a gradual depreciation against the USD, with occasional intervention from Chinese authorities to stabilize exchange rates.

CHF against USD

The CHF has appreciated against the USD over the past five years, driven by its safe-haven status and Switzerland's stable economy.

PKR against USD

The PKR has depreciated significantly against the USD, with substantial volatility due to economic challenges in Pakistan.

KWD against USD

The KWD has demonstrated relative stability against the USD, due to Kuwait's economic fundamentals and substantial foreign exchange reserves.

AED against USD

The AED has maintained a relatively stable position against the USD, due to its peg to the dollar and UAE's robust economic fundamentals

TRY against USD

The TRY has experienced considerable depreciation against the USD over the last five years, driven by domestic and international factors.

What is Forex Trading

Where you can lose money faster than you can say 'pip'!

Forex trading involves buying and selling currencies in the foreign exchange market to profit from fluctuations in exchange rates. Traders use various strategies, including technical analysis, fundamental analysis, and market news, to make informed decisions. The forex market operates 24 hours a day, five days a week, offering high liquidity and leverage. It's essential for traders to understand market dynamics and risks to manage their investments effectively.
